    Mombasa Plastic Prize Challenge

    Design researcher | Workshop Facilitator | Business design coach
    ×

    Project Overview

    The Mombasa Plastic Prize Challenge program targeted to encourage aspiring entrepreneurs (aged 18-25) to develop solutions that tackle marine plastic waste within Mombasa County’s informal settlements.

    Project Challenge

    To understand the context and needs of youth entrepreneurs/innovators, gather feedback on the prize design details, including program, incentives, eligibility, and selection criteria, in order to create a prize that is relevant and appealing to the target audience. Conduct a 6 month program that aimed to instill HCD skills to help young entrepreneurs in coming up with sustainable impactful solutions that address the challenges brought about by plastic waste in informal settlements in Mombasa County.

    My Contribution

    • Planned and coordinated field research.
    • Conducted and documented user interviews.
    • Synthesized research data to identify key user insights.
    • Drafted field research report.
    • Developed prototyping plan and user testing plans to help the innovators come up with prototypes and thoroughly test them out with the target market.
    • Facilitated workshop session on prototyping and user testing.
    • Coached teams on business model and pitching.
    • Managed stakeholder's communications across various teams.

    Project Outcome

    • Research report that gave a detailed summary of the plastic waste ecosystem, highlighting the different stakeholders and challenges within the ecosystem.
    • 14 innovative solutions emerged from the program that were capable of being scaled into functional businesses.
    • 14 tonnes of plastic collected during the program phase.
    • 97 strategic partnerships formed with the innovator teams and stakeholders in Mombasa County.
    • Cash prize award of 7 million Kenyan shillings to the top three.
    • More than 60 innovators were equipped with HCD skills and learned how to apply the methodology in finding pain points within their environment and using insights to come up with business ideas.
    • Development of an incubator program with 9 teams to progress their working prototypes into working sustainable businesses.

    Drawing Dreams Initiative

    Lead project designer | Lead researcher | Facilitator
    ×

    Project Overview

    The Road Trip for a Cause (RT4C) is a social impact travel program created by Drawing Dream Initiative (DDI), a youth-led Community-based Organization with footprints in Laikipia, Isiolo, and Samburu counties. The program aims to raise awareness and share information about menstrual health and hygiene management, sexual reproductive health and rights, and youth development. RT4C 2023 focused on promoting equity and understanding the value of education regarding sexual reproductive health, menstrual health, and the community's resilience to climate change.

    Project Challenge

    Utilizing Human-Centered Design (HCD) principles, create an immersive experience for the RT4C (name of the project/program) that aims to foster positive social impact by deeply understanding the Samburu way of life. Develop innovative strategies to enhance community engagement, ensuring maximum effectiveness and meaningful participation. Researching on the cultural heritage of Samburu people and their culture and traditions to have contextual information about their challenges.

    My Contribution

    • Led the discovery meeting with the stakeholders for needs assessment.
    • Developed a project plan.
    • Designed research plan and tools to be used during the project such as survey and interview guides.
    • Oversaw the research design.
    • Conducted user interviews.
    • Facilitated SHRH session with the participants.
    • Coordinated research activities.

    Project Outcome

    • A comprehensive and detailed project plan that guided more than 60 individuals who participated in the project delivery.
    • Very intuitive session plans that made delivery of SRH content to 220 learners of different age groups easy and engaging.
    • Engaged with more than 300 residents of Kilmatany village and conducted in-depth interviews with more than 10 residents.
    • Comprehensive understanding of the Samburu ways of living, cultural nuances, and their heritage.
    • Equipped participants with comprehensive discussion guides to facilitate structured interactions and explore specific areas of interest with the local community.
    • Identified opportunity areas for future programs to have a greater impact through active collaboration and input from local community members.

    SpaceAI

    Design researcher | Service designer
    ×

    Project Overview

    SpaceAI is a design and technology company that leverages research and a decentralized technology platform to facilitate service and data exchange across businesses through a digital network of designers, researchers and agents.

    Project Challenge

    To gain a comprehensive understanding of the challenges involved in last-mile data collection within the dairy value chain ecosystem. Our goal was to develop a user-centric digital platform that effectively captures and manages this crucial data, ensuring efficiency throughout the dairy value chain. Creating an intuitive digital platform easy to use by farmers and agents who aren't tech savvy. Challenge - creating a user-centric digital platform that provides last mile data on the dairy value chain

    My Contribution

    • Conducted desk research on the agricultural landscape with a focus on the dairy value chain and agricultural digital platforms.
    • Exploratory research on SMEs value chain to see how best SpaceAI can be used.
    • Generated key insights from the research.
    • Developed user flows for the system that guided farmers' onboarding and geo-location of properties.
    • Managed researched data and tools to ensure availability and collaboration.
    • Conducted design research to identify critical gaps and promising opportunities within the dairy value chain in Nandi County.
    • Created user flows for the proposed system, outlining the step-by-step interactions and processes involved.
    • Conducted interviews with a range of stakeholders, ensuring a comprehensive understanding of their perspectives, challenges, and aspirations within the dairy value chain.
    • Archived the research data collected, ensuring its proper organization, storage, and accessibility for future reference and analysis.

    Project Outcome

    • In-depth understanding of the dairy value chain ecosystem.
    • Mapped out stakeholders within the dairy value chain.
    • Digitized milk records for farmers and the dairy cooperatives.
    • Created intuitive user flows that made it easy to onboard farmers, last-mile agents, and within the value chain using easy-to-use conversational platforms.
    • Identified growth areas for the platform.
    • Established a research data bank, ensuring the systematic organization and storage of collected data and a central repository for research data.
    • User-centered platform that captures and stores last-mile data on the dairy value chain.
    • Connecting value creators within the dairy value chain such as transporters, dairy cooperatives, and financial services.

    Day of Adaptation - Minions of Disruption

    Design researcher
    ×

    Project Overview

    The project involved using Human Centered Design to adapt Minions of Disruption, a collaborative climate board game developed by Day of Adaptation (Netherlands) to the Kenyan context. We set out to attempt to develop an advocacy tool to engage societal actors, with a focus on climate justice and the inclusion of those groups in vulnerable situations.

    Project Challenge

    was to explore the acceptability of a climate game as an advocacy tool in a vastly different context than what the game was originally designed for Deepen understanding of the lifestyles and cultural assets of vulnerable groups residing in Kajiado, with the objective of adapting the Minions of Disruption as an advocacy tool for climate resilience that aligns with their unique context and needs.

    My Contribution

    • Conducted comprehensive desk research on the impacts of climate change on pastoralists residing in Kajiado, examining their existing climate resilience strategies.
    • Identified gaps in the desk research and actively participated in the development of field research methodologies, including crafting discussion guides and formulating survey questions.
    • Compiled a research report that effectively presented the findings, highlighted gaps in knowledge, and provided valuable insights derived from the research process.
    • Assisted in user testing and validation of the game.
    • Report writing.

    Project Outcome

    • Insight & ecosystem map report from the research.
    • Documented the impact of climate change and different resilience measures.
    • Obtained a deeper understanding of the communities residing in Kajiado, gaining insights into the specific contextualized effects of climate change on their lives.
    • Enhanced understanding of the community's knowledge and awareness of climate change, as well as their existing resilient measures to mitigate its impact.
    • Identified and explored the cultural assets and social dynamics within the communities, recognizing their significance in shaping their responses to climate change and fostering resilience.
